In memory of Rickie Makin

We're raising funds for causes that were special to Rickie, known to his friends and family as a kind and generous soul. He was a giver and a sharer in life. He had a simple philosophy: to make sure his time on Earth has been worthwhile, others benefited and shared some good fortune.


It was that simple for Rickie.

About Rickie

He was a sailor, a coder, a black belt in taekwondo, a surfer, a business owner, scuba diver and a traveller. Most importantly, he was a partner, a son, a brother and true friend to many people from all walks of life. Sadly taken too young in a tragic sailing accident on March 7th, 2020. We have unfortunately lost a wonderful man whilst participating in a sport he loved so much.


The world is a poorer place for his loss. We who loved Rickie were blessed having him in our lives even though it was for a very much shorter time then we find easy to accept.

About this fund

This fund was established to honour Rickie's memory and his generosity by continuing to support the causes that mattered to him. It's open to everyone to make a contribution and all are welcome to leave a message too. While we can, we also offer matching gifts. The causes were selected by his his mum, Kay, partner, Jodie and lifelong friend, Greg.


The fund is auspiced under the Australian Communities Foundation.

Frequently Asked Questions

More details on the operation of the fund can be found here.

Q: Where does the money go?

All money is paid directly into a Named Sub-Fund within the Australian Communities Foundation, a registered charity. ACF is Australia's only national community foundation. They operate a Public Ancillary Fund to provide administration, compliance, auditing and other governance services to philanthropists. Money held in trust before distribution to charities is invested responsibly..

Q: Are these gifts tax deductible?

Yes. The Australian Communities Foundation will issue donors a receipt via email which can be used for tax deductions. All money distributed to charities through ACF are themselves holding a tax-deductible status.

Q: What fees are involved?

The Australian Communities Foundation deducts a fee of 2.2% per annum on money held within the fund. This pays for their professional services including administration, auditing, compliance and related governance. This fee is inclusive of any costs they incur by payment providers. Further, a 0.37% per annum investment fee is charged on funds under management, to ensure money is not left idle. These funds are invested responsiby and have provided annualised return to charities in excess of 3% over the last five years.

For the Warrnambool College Prize in Rickie's memory, an intermediary charity called Schools Plus is required. This is because of the way the Australian Tax Office governs charities awarding scholarships directly to students. Schools Plus receives an additional 5% for setting up and operating the program.

Q: How were these causes selected?

A small group of people close to Rickie came together to select a portfolio of causes that reflect Rickie's values and life story. This includes Rickie's mum, Kay, partner, Jodie, and lifelong friend, Greg. ACF's in-house grant-making experts provided consultation services and recommended a balanced portfolio of high quality charities.

Q: I'd like to change my instructions. How do I edit them?

Please get in touch via email at gregory.hill+requiem@gmail.com and we'll sort it out.

Q: Can I make a recurring donation?

Yes. The Australian Communities Foundation handles recurring donations (weekly, monthly or quarterly). However, to ensure the correct calculation of matching funds, please contact via email at gregory.hill+requiem@gmail.com and we'll sort it out.

Q: How does the gift matching work?

The Named Sub-Fund will collect instructions from donors about how they would like their gift used. When directed to the whole fund, the balance of the fund will increase and be made available to match to subsequent donor's gifts. When directed to specific causes, the balance of the fund will reduce by that amount, dollar-for-dollar, and double the donation will be made to that cause. Matching will cease when the balance of the fund goes to zero. If a "whole fund" gift is then made, the balance will be positive. Requests to match gifts will then resume. Only donations made through this fund will be matched.
